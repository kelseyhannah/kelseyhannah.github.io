About Me

My name is Kelsey Hannah
and I like to do art.

Biography

Born near the geographic center of Tennessee, Kelsey Hannah spent her early childhood playing in dirt, climbing trees, collecting insects, whistling at birds and recording these experiences and creatures in the form of, at first, very inaccurate drawings. Driven inside her own mind by her shy personality and volatile home environment, she found an obsession with accurately depicting what she saw in the world around her. In 2011/2012 she won Best in Show for a self portrait done in pastel and graphite. After graduating from Riverdale High School in 2014, she was accepted into Middle Tennessee State University and will graduate in Fall of 2018 with a Bachelors of Arts in Japanese Language and Literature accompanied by a minor in art. Throughout her college career, Kelsey has accepted and fulfilled a wide range of commissions including portraiture to digital prints. In 2016/2018 she was commissioned by a small business based in Ohio called Shipping Hip to design patterns and post cards for stationary and polymailers. In Spring of 2018 her work was/ is shown at Moxie Art Supply and Just Love Coffee Roasters where it can still be seen today. Focusing primarily on oils, watercolor, and ink, she created paintings that elevate the minuscule and mundane into images of respect and dare she say, worship. Following her graduation in Fall, Kelsey will continue to work freelance and soon after pursue a Master of Fine Arts in Painting from the University of her choosing.

Artist Statement

Rooted in Naturalist philosophy, my work breeds flora and fauna and amalgamates these hybrids with sacred geometry and symbolism sourced from many faiths and spiritualities. Using art to explore the idea that we all acknowledge the presence and authority that the natural world exerts over us, I hope to provoke thought on the purpose of religion, itâ€™s origins and how it both connects and tears us from nature. By aspiring to draw attention to and educate my audience on the uncommon, endangered, or extinct species that we continue to exploit, I hope yo create simultaneous senses of solidarity with the natural world around us.
